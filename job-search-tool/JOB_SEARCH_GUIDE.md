# Teaching, Tutoring & Literacy Job Search Guide

A comprehensive guide for finding teaching, tutoring, and literacy education jobs - including the hidden ones that never make it to Indeed.

## Quick Start

```bash
# Install and run the search tool
pip3 install requests beautifulsoup4
python3 job_search.py --location "Your City, ST"

# Just get clickable links to open in your browser
python3 job_search.py --links-only --location "Your City, ST"

# See tutoring platforms where you can sign up today
python3 job_search.py --platforms

# Save results to a spreadsheet
python3 job_search.py --location "Your City, ST" --output results.csv
```

## The Hidden Job Market: Where Schools & Nonprofits Actually Post

Many teaching and literacy jobs never make it to Indeed. Here's where to find them:

### 1. Individual School District Career Pages

Most districts post jobs on their own websites first (sometimes exclusively). Search Google for:
```
"[your city] school district careers"
"[your city] public schools employment"
"[your county] school district jobs"
```

### 2. Charter School Networks

Charter schools often hire independently. Major networks:
- **KIPP** - kipp.org/careers
- **Uncommon Schools** - uncommonschools.org/careers
- **Achievement First** - achievementfirst.org/careers
- **Success Academy** - successacademies.org/careers
- **Teach For America** alumni network

### 3. Google Power Searches

Use these search strings to find hidden jobs (copy-paste into Google):

**Find jobs on school/org websites:**
```
"reading tutor" OR "literacy" (careers OR jobs OR "apply now") "Your City" -site:indeed.com -site:linkedin.com
```

**Find nonprofit literacy jobs:**
```
"literacy tutor" OR "reading specialist" (nonprofit OR "non-profit") (careers OR jobs) "Your City" -site:indeed.com
```

**Search .edu sites:**
```
site:.edu "reading" OR "literacy" OR "tutor" "position" OR "job" "Your City"
```

**Search .org sites:**
```
site:.org "reading tutor" OR "literacy coach" (careers OR jobs) "Your City"
```

**Find jobs on applicant tracking systems:**
```
site:applitrack.com "reading" OR "literacy" "Your State"
site:schooljobs.com "tutor" OR "reading"
site:governmentjobs.com "literacy" OR "reading tutor"
```

### 4. Local Organizations to Contact Directly

Many of these hire but don't post on major job boards:

- **Public libraries** - Many run literacy programs and hire tutors
- **Community colleges** - Adult education, ESL, developmental reading
- **Head Start programs** - Early childhood literacy
- **Boys & Girls Clubs** - After-school tutoring programs
- **YMCA/YWCA** - Youth education programs
- **United Way** - Often funds and staffs literacy initiatives
- **Reading Partners** - National nonprofit, local chapters
- **Literacy Volunteers of America** - Local chapters hire coordinators
- **Sylvan Learning Centers** - Always hiring tutors
- **Kumon** - Math and reading centers
- **Huntington Learning Centers** - Tutoring centers
- **Mathnasium** - Sometimes has reading programs too
- **Local churches/faith organizations** - Many run tutoring programs
- **After-school programs** - City/county recreation departments
- **Refugee resettlement agencies** - ESL/literacy needs

### 5. Literacy-Specific Organizations

- **Reading Is Fundamental (RIF)** - [rif.org/careers](https://www.rif.org/about-us/careers)
- **Beyond Basics** - [beyondbasics.org/careers](https://beyondbasics.org/careers/) - Provides free tutor training ($1,500 value)
- **Literacy Partners** - NYC-based adult literacy
- **ProLiteracy** - National literacy advocacy
- **Barbara Bush Foundation for Family Literacy**
- **National Center for Families Learning**
- **Room to Read** (international)

## Strategy: The Two-Track Approach

### Track 1: Immediate Income (Start This Week)
1. Sign up on **Wyzant** - set up your profile, start getting matched with students
2. Sign up on **Varsity Tutors** or **BookNook** as backup
3. List tutoring services on **Thumbtack** and **Care.com** for local clients
4. Contact **Sylvan/Kumon/Huntington** near you - they're almost always hiring

### Track 2: Full-Time Position (Ongoing Search)
1. Set up **daily email alerts** on SchoolSpring, Indeed, and LinkedIn
2. Run the `job_search.py` script weekly with your location
3. Check **individual school district websites** in your area monthly
4. Apply to **literacy nonprofits** (Idealist, Foundation List)
5. Use the **Google power searches** above weekly
6. Network: join local teacher Facebook groups, attend school board meetings

## Tips

1. **Apply early** - Education jobs often close quickly, especially at popular schools
2. **Tailor each application** - Mention the specific school/org and their literacy approach
3. **Get Orton-Gillingham or Science of Reading training** - These certifications are increasingly in demand and can significantly boost your competitiveness
4. **Substitute teach** - Great way to get your foot in the door at specific schools
5. **Volunteer first** - Library literacy programs and Reading Partners are good networking opportunities
6. **Join Facebook groups** - Search for "[your city] teachers" or "reading tutors" groups
7. **Check Craigslist** - Parents sometimes post tutoring needs in the "education" or "gigs" section
