## Emerging Preachers & Teachers for the Fresh Text Podcast

Based on extensive research, here are recommended emerging voices who would be strong guest fits for the Fresh Text Podcast. These are people who combine pastoral ministry with theological depth, many of whom are active in the Wesleyan/holiness tradition and/or engage with the Revised Common Lectionary.

---

### 1. Rev. Alicia McClintic
- **Church/Institution:** Lead Pastor, Selah Church of the Nazarene, Selah, WA
- **Denomination:** Church of the Nazarene
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** McClintic co-hosts *A Plain Account*, a free online Wesleyan lectionary commentary and podcast -- making her a direct peer to what Fresh Text does. She preaches from the RCL weekly through a Wesleyan-holiness lens. She holds an MA in Theology and Culture from Fuller and was a featured preacher at the 2024 NTS Hugh C. Benner Preachers' Conference. She is also a mentor to women in ministry and deeply committed to holiness preaching. Her lectionary work makes her an almost perfect match for Fresh Text's format.

### 2. Dr. Shawna Songer Gaines
- **Church/Institution:** Lead Pastor, Trevecca Community Church of the Nazarene, Nashville, TN
- **Denomination:** Church of the Nazarene
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** A D.Min. holder and ordained Nazarene elder, Songer Gaines is the author of *Pastor as Midwife* and co-author of *A Seat at the Table*. Trevecca president Dan Boone called her "one of our outstanding preachers." She was a featured speaker at the 2024 NTS Preachers' Conference. Her combination of pastoral leadership at a Nazarene university church and published theological reflection on ministry makes her an ideal pastor-scholar guest.

### 3. Rev. Dr. Andrea Summers
- **Church/Institution:** Campus Pastor & Dean of Spiritual Formation, Indiana Wesleyan University
- **Denomination:** The Wesleyan Church
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** Summers has already appeared on Fresh Text (discussing Ruth 3), so she has a proven relationship with the show. She holds an M.Div. from Asbury Theological Seminary and a D.Min. from Candler/Emory. She previously directed Sacred Alliance (advancing women in Wesleyan ministry) and is a mainstage speaker at the 2026 WHWC conference. She preaches regularly at IWU chapel and is deeply embedded in the Wesleyan ecosystem.

### 4. Rev. Kris Sledge
- **Church/Institution:** Founding & Lead Pastor, The Journey Church (UMC), Harrisburg, PA
- **Denomination:** United Methodist Church
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Sledge is a Wesley Theological Seminary graduate who planted a thriving, diverse UMC congregation where 65% are under 40 and 45% are people of color. He co-authored *Followers Under 40* (2025) with Rachel Gilmore and is a sought-after speaker at UMC annual conferences on reaching younger generations. He represents the young, diverse, urban face of Methodist ministry and could bring a fresh generational perspective to lectionary preaching.

### 5. Rachel Gilmore
- **Church/Institution:** Director of New and Vital Faith, Desert Southwest Annual Conference, UMC; Co-pastor, Central UMC, Phoenix, AZ
- **Denomination:** United Methodist Church
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** Gilmore is a church planter, podcaster, and co-author of *Followers Under 40*. She planted a church in Virginia Beach, cultivated fresh expressions in jails and feeding ministries, and now leads new church development for an entire UMC conference. Her blend of entrepreneurial ministry and deep Methodist roots would bring energy to lectionary discussions, especially around how scripture speaks to next-generation faith communities.

### 6. Johnathan Arnold
- **Church/Institution:** Senior Pastor, Redeemer Wesleyan Church, Mount Pleasant Mills, PA; President, Holy Joys
- **Denomination:** The Wesleyan Church
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Arnold is at the center of the "Wesley is fire now" movement profiled by Christianity Today in September 2024. Through Holy Joys (25,000+ podcast downloads), he is recovering classical Methodist theology for a new generation. He is authoring a forthcoming catechism (*The Whole Counsel of God*) and publishes in Firebrand Magazine. As a young pastor-theologian leading the Wesleyan theological revival, he'd bring deep doctrinal engagement to lectionary conversations.

### 7. David Fry
- **Church/Institution:** Senior Pastor, Frankfort Bible Holiness Church, Frankfort, IN; Chairman, Holy Joys
- **Denomination:** Bible Methodist Connection of Churches
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Fry holds degrees from God's Bible School and College, Wesley Biblical Seminary, and a Ph.D. from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School. He serves on the Holy Joys board and is quoted in the Christianity Today "Wesley is fire now" article on Wesleyan ecclesiology. His scholarly credentials in the holiness tradition combined with local church pastoring make him a strong pastor-scholar voice, and his Indiana location is close to the IWU orbit.

### 8. Dr. Sterling M. Allen
- **Church/Institution:** Senior Pastor, Cornerstone Global Methodist Church, Houston, TX; Adjunct Faculty, Baylor Truett Theological Seminary
- **Denomination:** Global Methodist Church
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Allen is a leading Charles Wesley scholar who edited the GMC hymnal *O for a Heart to Praise My God* and the Seedbed Worship Planner 2025-2026, which follows an adapted lectionary. He led worship at the GMC's founding General Conference and holds a Doctor of Worship in liturgics and hymnology. His deep integration of Wesleyan hymnody, lectionary-based worship planning, and pastoral ministry makes him a uniquely qualified guest for discussing how scripture shapes worship.

### 9. Rev. Brian Choi
- **Church/Institution:** Church Planter & Lead Pastor, Beta Church, Mason, OH
- **Denomination:** Global Methodist Church
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Choi represents the next generation of GMC church planters, focused specifically on equipping Gen Z and Gen Alpha. He is planting in suburban Ohio and brings a fresh perspective on what Methodist church planting looks like post-denominational split. His youth and entrepreneurial energy would bring a different voice to lectionary discussions -- how emerging congregations engage with scripture.

### 10. Emma Winchester
- **Church/Institution:** Staff, Spirit & Truth (renewal ministry in the Wesleyan/Methodist tradition)
- **Denomination:** Global Methodist Church / Wesleyan-Methodist
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** Winchester preached the opening session of the GMC's 2:42 Conference (a gathering for young pastors and seminary students) in 2025. She works with Spirit & Truth, which also publishes Firebrand Magazine. As a young woman preaching to the next generation of Methodist leaders, she represents exactly the kind of emerging voice Fresh Text could elevate.

### 11. Dr. Jason Buchwalter
- **Church/Institution:** Pastor, Fulton Heartland Church of the Nazarene; Chaplain, Westminster College
- **Denomination:** Church of the Nazarene
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Buchwalter is a co-host of *A Plain Account*'s "Sacred & Small" series, which focuses on lectionary preaching in small church contexts -- a perspective that would resonate with many Fresh Text listeners. His dual role as small-church pastor and college chaplain gives him a unique vantage point on how the lectionary functions across different ministry contexts within the Wesleyan-holiness tradition.

### 12. Dr. Elaine Bernius
- **Church/Institution:** Professor of Biblical Studies, Indiana Wesleyan University
- **Denomination:** The Wesleyan Church
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** Bernius has already appeared on Fresh Text (discussing Ruth 2), and students describe her as "a very good mix between pastor/professor." She holds a Ph.D. from Hebrew Union College and teaches Old Testament/Biblical Studies at IWU. Her scholarly expertise in Hebrew Bible combined with her pastoral sensibility and IWU connection makes her a natural recurring guest, particularly for Old Testament lectionary passages.

### 13. Rev. Althea Taylor
- **Church/Institution:** District Superintendent, Hawaii Pacific District, Church of the Nazarene
- **Denomination:** Church of the Nazarene
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** Taylor is an ordained Nazarene elder with 20+ years of pastoral leadership, formerly Executive Pastor at Bronx Bethany Church of the Nazarene. She preached at the 2024 NTS Preachers' Conference and has deep experience in compassionate ministries, immigration justice, and urban ministry. Her move from Bronx pastoral ministry to district leadership in Hawaii gives her a wide-angle view of preaching across diverse contexts.

### 14. Dr. Chris Lohrstorfer
- **Church/Institution:** Associate Professor of Wesleyan Theology & Interim VP of Academic Affairs, Wesley Biblical Seminary; Senior Pastor, Hinds Independent Methodist Church, Raymond, MS
- **Denomination:** Congregational Methodist Church
- **Why he'd be a great fit:** Lohrstorfer is quoted in the Christianity Today article on the Wesleyan revival, speaking about how Wesleyan ecclesiology offers what contemporary culture is searching for. He is both a seminary professor and a local church pastor of 25+ years, and his doctoral work focused on Wesley's doctrine of sin. He could bring rich Wesleyan theological depth to lectionary discussions.

### 15. Rev. Joanna DeWolf
- **Church/Institution:** Superintendent, East Michigan Conference; Lead Pastor, Lansing Central Free Methodist Church
- **Denomination:** Free Methodist Church
- **Why she'd be a great fit:** DeWolf represents the Free Methodist voice in the Wesleyan family, serving in dual roles as conference superintendent and local church pastor. She writes about "igniting a Spirit-fueled movement" and developing next-generation leaders. The Free Methodist tradition is underrepresented in many preaching conversations, and her leadership role could bring that perspective to Fresh Text's audience.

### Bonus Pipeline: Where to Find More Emerging Names

- **Order of the FLAME** (World Methodist Evangelism): This invitation-only program annually nominates clergy under 45 from across the Wesleyan-Methodist family. The 2026 gathering is March 9 at Epworth by the Sea, GA. FLAME alumni would be excellent guest candidates. ([worldmethodist.org/flame](https://worldmethodist.org/flame/))
- **GMC 2:42 Conference**: Annual gathering for young Global Methodist pastors and seminarians, now in its second year. ([globalmethodist.org](https://www.globalmethodist.org/))
- **Seedbed Fellowships & New Room Conference**: Seedbed actively cultivates Gen Z and young adult pastor cohorts. ([seedbed.com/fellowships](https://seedbed.com/fellowships/))
- **NTS Hugh C. Benner Preachers' Conference**: Annual event at Nazarene Theological Seminary featuring rising Nazarene preachers. ([ntspraxis.org](https://www.ntspraxis.org/))
- **A Plain Account contributors**: This Wesleyan lectionary commentary site draws essays from pastors and scholars across the Wesleyan family -- its contributor list is a rich source of potential guests. ([aplainaccount.org](https://www.aplainaccount.org/))
- **Firebrand Magazine writers**: Young Wesleyan-Methodist theologians publishing here would be natural Fresh Text guests. ([firebrandmag.com](https://firebrandmag.com/))
