## Recommended High-Profile Guests for the Fresh Text Podcast

Below are 15 potential guests organized by category, with rationale and accessibility notes.

---

### TIER 1: Marquee Names (High Impact, Harder to Book)

**1. N.T. Wright** -- Former Bishop of Durham; Research Professor Emeritus, University of St Andrews
- **Why:** The most recognized biblical scholar alive today. He hosts his own podcast ("Ask NT Wright Anything"), regularly does guest appearances on other shows, and has deep lectionary awareness from his Anglican/liturgical background. His work on Paul, the Gospels, and the kingdom of God resonates strongly with Wesleyan theology. He is scheduled to be at Truett Seminary in Houston in June 2026, which could be a booking opportunity.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate-to-difficult. He does many podcast appearances, but his schedule is packed. Having a connection through Princeton (where Beverly Gaventa taught) or through Anglican/Wesleyan networks could help. Best approached through his agent or NTWrightPage.com contacts.

**2. Will Willimon** -- Professor of the Practice of Christian Ministry, Duke Divinity School; retired United Methodist Bishop
- **Why:** Named one of the "Twelve Most Effective Preachers in the English-speaking world" by Baylor University. A United Methodist bishop who has written nearly 100 books. He is a prolific podcast guest (recently appeared on the Lewis Center podcast and The Last Service Podcast in 2025). He preaches from the lectionary and is deeply rooted in the Wesleyan tradition -- a near-perfect fit for Fresh Text.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. Willimon is generous with his time for ministry-focused podcasts and is still actively guest-preaching at Methodist churches. He would likely say yes, especially given the IWU/Wesleyan connection.

**3. Amy-Jill Levine** -- Rabbi Stanley M. Kessler Distinguished Professor, Hartford International University; University Professor Emerita, Vanderbilt
- **Why:** A Jewish New Testament scholar whose books on the parables (*Short Stories by Jesus*), the Sermon on the Mount, and the Gospel of John are massively popular in mainline Protestant churches. She writes Abingdon Press Bible study series that are staples in Methodist congregations. United Methodists have openly embraced her work. Her unique perspective -- a Jewish scholar illuminating Christian texts -- would make for a riveting Fresh Text episode.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate. She has given close to 1,000 lectures and is very active on the speaking circuit. She has strong Methodist church connections through Abingdon/Cokesbury publishing.

**4. Fleming Rutledge** -- Episcopal priest, preacher, and author
- **Why:** Widely recognized as one of the finest lectionary preachers in the English-speaking world. Her book *Means of Grace* provides weekly sermons based on Revised Common Lectionary readings. Her *The Crucifixion* won Christianity Today's Book of the Year. Her approach to preaching -- centering God's agency rather than human moralism -- would generate rich conversation with John Drury.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate. She is semi-retired but still active in preaching and speaking. She has guest-preached at Duke Chapel, Washington National Cathedral, and Harvard Memorial Church.

---

### TIER 2: Scholar-Communicators with Strong Wesleyan/Methodist Ties

**5. Scot McKnight** -- Professor of New Testament, Northern Seminary
- **Why:** Author of 50+ books, hosts the Kingdom Roots podcast, and has deep ties to Seedbed (the Wesleyan publishing platform). His *The King Jesus Gospel* is widely read in Wesleyan circles, and he has contributed to Seedbed's Seven Minute Seminary. An ordained Anglican deacon with Anabaptist leanings, his theology overlaps significantly with Wesleyan emphases on sanctification and kingdom living.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. McKnight is extremely generous with podcast appearances and would likely appreciate the Wesleyan-lectionary angle. He appeared on Theology in the Raw and many other shows.

**6. Joel B. Green** -- Professor of New Testament Interpretation, Fuller Theological Seminary
- **Why:** An ordained United Methodist elder who adheres to Wesleyan-Arminian theology. Former provost at both Asbury Theological Seminary and Fuller. Author of 55+ books including the landmark *Gospel of Luke* commentary. He literally wrote *Reading Scripture as Wesleyans* (Abingdon, 2010). He also appeared on Nijay Gupta's Engaging Scripture podcast. A Wesleyan scholar of the highest caliber.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate-to-high. His Asbury and UMC connections make this a natural fit. The IWU/Wesleyan network should provide a warm introduction.

**7. David deSilva** -- Trustees' Distinguished Professor of New Testament and Greek, Ashland Theological Seminary
- **Why:** An ordained elder (now Global Methodist Church), deSilva is a premier New Testament scholar in the Wesleyan orbit. His *Introduction to the New Testament* is a standard seminary textbook. He has written *Praying with John Wesley* and is a Working Preacher contributor. He also contributed the Wesleyan perspective in *Five Views on the Gospel*.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. He is at a smaller seminary and likely very accessible. His Working Preacher connection and Wesleyan ordination make this a natural pairing.

**8. Joy J. Moore** -- Former President, Northern Seminary; former Professor of Biblical Preaching, Luther Seminary
- **Why:** An ordained United Methodist elder, former president of the Wesleyan Theological Society (2020-2022), and former faculty at Wesley Seminary at Indiana Wesleyan University (2017-2018). She has taught at Fuller, Duke Divinity, and Asbury. She was a co-host of the Sermon Brainwave podcast. Her expertise in homiletics and her IWU history make her an incredibly natural guest.
- **Accessibility:** VERY HIGH. She literally taught at IWU's Wesley Seminary. The existing relationship should make booking straightforward.

---

### TIER 3: Rising Stars and Podcast Cross-Promotion Opportunities

**9. Esau McCaulley** -- Jonathan Blanchard Associate Professor of New Testament, Wheaton College
- **Why:** His *Reading While Black* won Christianity Today's Book of the Year and he is a contributing opinion writer for the New York Times. An Anglican priest with a heart for the Black church preaching tradition, he brings a powerful voice on how Scripture speaks to justice, race, and hope. He is also a senior editor for Holy Post Media.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate. He is in high demand but does many podcast appearances. His scholarly credentials (Ph.D., St. Andrews) combined with his pastoral and public voice make him a compelling draw for Fresh Text's audience.

**10. Nijay Gupta** -- Professor of New Testament, Northern Seminary
- **Why:** Hosts two podcasts (Engaging Scripture and Slow Theology), co-edits a book series with Scot McKnight, and is editor-in-chief of the *Bulletin for Biblical Research*. His book *Strange Religion* is getting wide attention. As a podcast host himself, he understands the medium and would be an engaging conversationalist. Cross-promotion potential with his Engaging Scripture podcast audience.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. As a fellow podcast host, he is very open to cross-appearances. Northern Seminary connections through McKnight and Moore create multiple warm-introduction paths.

**11. Karoline Lewis** -- Marbury E. Anderson Chair of Biblical Preaching, Luther Seminary
- **Why:** Co-host of the Sermon Brainwave podcast (the most direct competitor/complement to Fresh Text), a leading voice in lectionary preaching, and author of *A Lay Preacher's Guide* and the John commentary in the Fortress Biblical Preaching series. A cross-over episode could introduce both audiences to each other.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate-to-high. She is very active in the preaching podcast space and regularly does conferences and guest appearances. The shared lectionary-preaching mission creates a natural collaboration opportunity.

**12. Matt Skinner** -- Asher O. and Carrie Nasby Professor of New Testament, Luther Seminary
- **Why:** Co-host of Sermon Brainwave and a Working Preacher contributor. His scholarship on Acts and Matthew is widely respected, and his Dean described his Working Preacher contributions as making "top-end New Testament scholarship available around the globe." Like Lewis, a cross-promotion opportunity.
- **Accessibility:** Moderate-to-high. He does regular podcast work and conference speaking.

---

### TIER 4: Wesleyan Movement Leaders with Built-In Audiences

**13. J.D. Walt** -- Founder and "Sower-in-Chief," Seedbed
- **Why:** Hosts the daily Seedbed "Wake-Up Call" podcast and leads the New Room Conference. As the driving force behind Seedbed (which publishes the Seedbed Worship Planner for Lectionary Year A, 2025-2026), he has a massive built-in Wesleyan audience. A cross-promotion would expose Fresh Text to the entire Seedbed/New Room network.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. Seedbed and IWU both serve the Wesleyan world. This is a natural partnership conversation.

**14. Timothy Tennent** -- Distinguished Scholar of World Christianity, John Wesley Institute; former President, Asbury Theological Seminary
- **Why:** One of the most prominent institutional leaders in the Wesleyan movement. His transition from Asbury's presidency to the John Wesley Institute signals his continuing influence. He publishes through Seedbed and his annual lecture in Washington, D.C. draws wide attention. He brings a global Christianity perspective to Scripture that would enrich any lectionary conversation.
- **Accessibility:** HIGH. The Asbury-IWU-Wesleyan network makes this very approachable.

**15. Tim Mackie** -- Co-founder and Lead Scholar, BibleProject
- **Why:** BibleProject has over 30 million YouTube subscribers and its podcast is one of the most popular Bible-focused shows in the world. Mackie holds a Ph.D. in Hebrew Bible from UW-Madison and is a gifted communicator who makes complex scholarship accessible. While not specifically Wesleyan or lectionary-focused, his approach to reading the Bible as a unified narrative would resonate with Fresh Text's audience, and cross-promotion with BibleProject's audience would be transformational for Fresh Text's reach.
- **Accessibility:** Difficult but not impossible. Mackie does select guest appearances. He would need to see alignment with BibleProject's mission. An Old Testament lectionary text episode could be a compelling pitch.
