# Recommended Guest Scholars for the Fresh Text Podcast

Below are 20 scholars from traditions **outside** the Wesleyan/Methodist orbit who would be natural fits for the Fresh Text Podcast, given its lectionary focus, ecumenical spirit, and pastor-scholar audience.

---

## LUTHERAN (ELCA)

### 1. Karoline M. Lewis
- **Institution:** Luther Seminary, St. Paul, MN
- **Tradition:** Evangelical Lutheran Church in America (ELCA)
- **Expertise:** New Testament (especially the Gospel of John), biblical preaching, homiletics
- **Notable Work:** Co-host of the *Sermon Brainwave* podcast; author of *John: Fortress Biblical Preaching Commentaries*, *SHE: Five Keys to Unlock the Power of Women in Ministry*, and *Preaching the Word*; regular "Dear Working Preacher" columnist
- **Why She Fits:** Lewis is quite possibly the single most natural fit on this entire list. She lives at the intersection of biblical scholarship and sermon preparation every week on *Sermon Brainwave*. Her format -- professors discussing upcoming lectionary texts and swapping preaching ideas -- is almost identical to Fresh Text's model. She would understand the assignment instinctively.

### 2. Rolf A. Jacobson
- **Institution:** Luther Seminary, St. Paul, MN
- **Tradition:** ELCA
- **Expertise:** Old Testament, Psalms, humor in preaching
- **Notable Work:** Co-developer of the Narrative Lectionary; former co-host of *Sermon Brainwave*; author of *The Book of Psalms* (NICOT, with deClaisse-Walford and Tanner) and *Divine Laughter: Preaching and the Serious Business of Humor*
- **Why He Fits:** Jacobson brings an infectious energy and wit to lectionary conversation. His deep Old Testament expertise would complement Fresh Text's discussions of Hebrew Bible lections, and his years on *Sermon Brainwave* mean he knows the podcast-as-sermon-prep format cold. He also has a gift for making scholarship accessible to pastors.

### 3. Troy M. Troftgruben
- **Institution:** Wartburg Theological Seminary, Dubuque, IA
- **Tradition:** ELCA
- **Expertise:** New Testament (Luke-Acts, Matthew), lectionary preaching
- **Notable Work:** Author of *Rooted and Renewing* and *Bearing Witness in Acts and Today*; contributor to *Sundays and Seasons: Preaching*; leads lectionary year workshops for ELCA synods; currently writing a Luke commentary for the NICNT series
- **Why He Fits:** Troftgruben is a pastor-turned-professor who regularly leads lectionary preaching conferences for synods and actively writes lectionary prep materials. His pastoral sensibility and Luke-Acts expertise (especially relevant in Year C) would make him a grounded, practical conversation partner.

## ANGLICAN / EPISCOPAL

### 4. Wil Gafney
- **Institution:** Brite Divinity School, Fort Worth, TX
- **Tradition:** Episcopal priest; formerly ordained in the AME Zion Church
- **Expertise:** Hebrew Bible, womanist interpretation, lectionary design
- **Notable Work:** Author of the four-volume *A Women's Lectionary for the Whole Church*; *Womanist Midrash: A Reintroduction to Women of the Torah and the Throne*; *Daughters of Miriam: Women Prophets in Ancient Israel*
- **Why She Fits:** Gafney has literally reimagined the lectionary itself. A conversation with her about how texts get selected, what gets left out, and how preachers can recover overlooked voices in familiar passages would be a landmark episode. Her cross-traditional background (AME Zion roots, Episcopal priesthood, Disciples-affiliated seminary) embodies ecumenism.

### 5. Carolyn J. Sharp
- **Institution:** Yale Divinity School
- **Tradition:** Episcopal
- **Expertise:** Old Testament/Hebrew Bible, homiletics, prophetic literature
- **Notable Work:** Co-editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary series; author of commentaries on Micah, Jeremiah, and Joshua; founding series editor of the T&T Clark Library of Homiletics; contributor to Working Preacher
- **Why She Fits:** Sharp holds the rare distinction of being tenured in two disciplines at Yale (Hebrew Bible, then homiletics). She thinks professionally about how ancient texts become sermons. As a co-editor of *Connections*, she has shaped how thousands of pastors approach the RCL each week.

### 6. A. Katherine Grieb
- **Institution:** Virginia Theological Seminary
- **Tradition:** Episcopal
- **Expertise:** New Testament (especially Paul/Romans), theological interpretation of Scripture
- **Notable Work:** *The Story of Romans: A Narrative Defense of God's Righteousness* (widely used in seminaries); Director of the Center for Anglican Communion Studies; one of the Archbishop of Canterbury's "Six Preachers"
- **Why She Fits:** Grieb's narrative approach to Paul resonates with Wesleyan emphasis on Scripture's transformative power. Her book on Romans is a model of making Pauline theology accessible for preaching, and her ecumenical work across the Anglican Communion shows comfort with diverse theological conversation partners.

### 7. Esau McCaulley
- **Institution:** Wheaton College
- **Tradition:** Anglican (ACNA); founding pastor of All Saints Anglican Church, Naperville, IL
- **Expertise:** New Testament, African American biblical interpretation, public theology
- **Notable Work:** *Reading While Black: African American Biblical Interpretation as an Exercise in Hope* (Christianity Today Book of the Year); editor of *New Testament in Color*; contributing opinion writer for the *New York Times*
- **Why He Fits:** McCaulley is one of the most prominent younger biblical scholars in American Christianity. His Anglican liturgical formation, pastoral experience, and commitment to reading Scripture as a source of hope for marginalized communities align beautifully with Wesleyan sensibilities about Scripture's social and personal transformative power.

## PRESBYTERIAN / REFORMED

### 8. Anna Carter Florence
- **Institution:** Columbia Theological Seminary, Decatur, GA
- **Tradition:** Presbyterian Church (USA)
- **Expertise:** Homiletics, testimony as preaching, creative Scripture engagement
- **Notable Work:** *Preaching as Testimony*; *Rehearsing Scripture* (based on her Beecher Lectures at Yale); *A is for Alabaster: 52 Reflections on the Stories of Scripture*; Peter Marshall Professor of Preaching
- **Why She Fits:** Carter Florence's creative, text-centered approach to preaching -- helping preachers "rehearse" their way into Scripture -- would generate a lively conversation on the show. Her emphasis on testimony and her well-known gift for making texts come alive is perfectly suited to a sermon-starter format.

### 9. Cynthia L. Rigby
- **Institution:** Austin Presbyterian Theological Seminary
- **Tradition:** Presbyterian Church (USA)
- **Expertise:** Systematic theology, feminist theology, doctrine made accessible for preaching
- **Notable Work:** Co-editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary; *Holding Faith: A Practical Introduction to Christian Faith*; co-editor (with Beverly Gaventa) of *Blessed One: Protestant Perspectives on Mary*; Excellence in Theological Education Award from PC(USA)
- **Why She Fits:** Rigby is known for making theology come alive for non-specialists -- the *Dallas Morning News* called her "one of the great theologians of our time." Her collaboration with Beverly Gaventa (a past Fresh Text guest) and her co-editorship of *Connections* place her squarely in the lectionary-preaching ecosystem. She brings the systematic theology angle that enriches exegetical conversation.

### 10. Thomas G. Long
- **Institution:** Candler School of Theology, Emory University (Emeritus)
- **Tradition:** Presbyterian Church (USA)
- **Expertise:** Homiletics, biblical interpretation for preaching
- **Notable Work:** Co-editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary; *The Witness of Preaching*; *Preaching from Memory to Hope* (Beecher Lectures); commentaries on Hebrews and Matthew; named one of the twelve most effective preachers in the English-speaking world by Baylor University
- **Why He Fits:** Long is arguably the most influential homiletician of the past generation. His thinking about how preachers move from text to sermon has shaped the field. A conversation with him would offer Fresh Text listeners a masterclass in the craft, and his irenic Presbyterian sensibility pairs well with Wesleyan warmth.

### 11. Raj Nadella
- **Institution:** Columbia Theological Seminary
- **Tradition:** Presbyterian Church (USA)
- **Expertise:** New Testament, postcolonial biblical interpretation, economic justice in the Gospels
- **Notable Work:** *Dialogue Not Dogma: Many Voices in the Gospel of Luke*; contributor to Working Preacher, *Sojourners*, and *The Christian Century*; co-editing *The Oxford Handbook of the Bible, Race and Diaspora*
- **Why He Fits:** Nadella brings a global, postcolonial lens to familiar lectionary texts that can open fresh angles for preachers. His focus on Luke's economic ethics would be especially rich for Year C episodes. He is already embedded in the lectionary-commentary world through Working Preacher.

## CATHOLIC

### 12. Amy-Jill Levine
- **Institution:** Hartford International University (formerly Vanderbilt Divinity School)
- **Tradition:** Jewish (Orthodox) -- but deeply embedded in Christian lectionary and preaching worlds
- **Expertise:** Historical Jesus, parables, Jewish context of the New Testament, Jewish-Christian relations
- **Notable Work:** *The Jewish Annotated New Testament* (co-editor); *Short Stories by Jesus: The Enigmatic Parables of a Controversial Rabbi*; Abingdon Press lectionary-aligned studies for churches: *Light of the World* (Advent), *Entering the Passion of Jesus* (Lent), *Signs and Wonders*
- **Why She Fits:** Levine is a Jewish scholar who has become one of the most beloved voices in mainline Protestant sermon preparation. Her Abingdon studies are already used in Wesleyan and Methodist congregations. She illuminates the Jewish world of Jesus in ways that transform how preachers handle Gospel lectionary texts. A Fresh Text episode with her would be unforgettable.

### 13. Mary Healy
- **Institution:** Sacred Heart Major Seminary, Detroit
- **Tradition:** Roman Catholic
- **Expertise:** New Testament (Mark, Hebrews, Genesis), Catholic biblical interpretation, charismatic renewal
- **Notable Work:** General editor and author of volumes in the *Catholic Commentary on Sacred Scripture*; first woman appointed to the Pontifical Biblical Commission; member of the Pentecostal-Catholic International Dialogue; co-author with Protestant evangelist Randy Clark of *The Spiritual Gifts Handbook*
- **Why She Fits:** Healy is a rare Catholic scholar who actively collaborates with Protestants and Pentecostals. Her emphasis on the Holy Spirit and spiritual gifts resonates with Wesleyan pneumatology. Since Catholics and Protestants share a lectionary structure, her perspective on the same Sunday texts from a Catholic vantage point would be genuinely enriching for Fresh Text listeners.

## DISCIPLES OF CHRIST

### 14. Lance Pape
- **Institution:** Brite Divinity School, Fort Worth, TX (Disciples of Christ)
- **Tradition:** Christian Church (Disciples of Christ)
- **Expertise:** Homiletical theory, biblical hermeneutics and preaching
- **Notable Work:** *The Scandal of Having Something to Say: Ricoeur and the Possibility of Postliberal Preaching*; *New Proclamation Year C 2013: Easter Through Christ the King* (lectionary preaching resource); Granville and Erline Walker Associate Professor of Homiletics
- **Why He Fits:** Pape brings deep homiletical theory to bear on weekly preaching practice. His Disciples of Christ tradition shares the Wesleyan emphasis on unity and ecumenism, and his lectionary preaching resources show he is already thinking about the same texts Fresh Text covers.

## HISTORICALLY BLACK DENOMINATIONS / BLACK CHURCH SCHOLARS

### 15. Luke A. Powery
- **Institution:** Duke University Chapel / Duke Divinity School
- **Tradition:** Baptist heritage; serves in a broadly ecumenical role as Dean of Duke Chapel
- **Expertise:** Homiletics, pneumatology, African diaspora preaching traditions, music and preaching
- **Notable Work:** General editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary; *Becoming Human: The Holy Spirit and the Rhetoric of Race* (RCA Book of the Year); *Spirit Speech: Lament and Celebration in Preaching*; *Dem Dry Bones: Preaching, Death, and Hope*
- **Why He Fits:** Powery's combination of lectionary expertise (as a *Connections* general editor) and pneumatological focus aligns remarkably well with Wesleyan theology's emphasis on the Holy Spirit. His work on how the Spirit moves through preaching would produce a rich conversation, and his Advent/Lent reflections rooted in spirituals offer a distinctive homiletical voice.

### 16. Cleophus J. LaRue
- **Institution:** Princeton Theological Seminary
- **Tradition:** National Baptist Convention of America
- **Expertise:** African American preaching, homiletical theory and practice
- **Notable Work:** *The Heart of Black Preaching*; *I Believe I'll Testify: The Art of African American Preaching*; *Power in the Pulpit*; Francis Landey Patton Professor of Homiletics
- **Why He Fits:** LaRue's scholarship on Black preaching's distinctive biblical hermeneutic -- viewing God as practically involved in believers' lives -- echoes Wesleyan emphases on God's active, transforming grace. His Princeton affiliation (alongside past Fresh Text guest Beverly Gaventa) and his work helping preachers across traditions learn from Black homiletical tradition make him a compelling guest.

### 17. Raquel St. Clair Lettsome
- **Institution:** Eden Theological Seminary, St. Louis, MO
- **Tradition:** African Methodist Episcopal (AME) Church
- **Expertise:** New Testament, womanist biblical interpretation, Gospel of Mark
- **Notable Work:** *Call and Consequences: A Womanist Reading of Mark*; forthcoming Mark commentary for the *Interpretation* series (WJK, 2026); first African American to earn a PhD in New Testament from Princeton Seminary
- **Why She Fits:** As an AME elder, Lettsome comes from a tradition that shares deep Methodist and Wesleyan roots with the IWU audience. Her womanist reading of Mark and her forthcoming *Interpretation* commentary would be directly relevant to lectionary preaching in Year B. Her Eden Theological Seminary appointment (UCC-affiliated) adds another ecumenical dimension.

## ECUMENICAL / INTERDENOMINATIONAL

### 18. Joel B. Green
- **Institution:** Fuller Theological Seminary
- **Tradition:** United Methodist (ordained elder), working at an interdenominational seminary
- **Expertise:** New Testament (especially Luke-Acts), theological interpretation, neuroscience and theology
- **Notable Work:** General editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary series; *The Gospel of Luke* (NICNT); editor of the New International Commentary on the New Testament; formerly provost of Asbury Theological Seminary
- **Why He Fits:** Though technically a United Methodist elder, Green's career has been overwhelmingly interdenominational (Fuller, Asbury, American Baptist Seminary of the West). His co-editorship of *Connections* and his Luke commentary make him a premier lectionary resource. His Asbury connection means he already understands the Wesleyan world intimately while bringing a broader perspective.

### 19. Debie Thomas
- **Institution:** St. Mark's Episcopal Church, Palo Alto, CA; *The Christian Century*
- **Tradition:** Episcopal
- **Expertise:** Lectionary reflection writing, creative nonfiction, spiritual formation
- **Notable Work:** Columnist for *The Christian Century*; former staff writer for *Journey with Jesus* (2014-2022), where her weekly lectionary essays doubled readership; author of *Into the Mess and Other Jesus Stories* and *A Faith of Many Rooms*
- **Why She Fits:** Thomas represents the world of popular lectionary writing that pastors actually use week-to-week. Her essays are read by thousands of preachers for sermon inspiration. Having a practitioner of the lectionary-reflection genre (rather than a traditional academic) would offer Fresh Text a different kind of conversation -- focused on the craft of turning a text into a living word for a congregation.

### 20. Matthew Myer Boulton
- **Institution:** SALT Project (production company and lectionary commentary)
- **Tradition:** United Church of Christ (UCC); academic formation at Harvard Divinity School and University of Chicago Divinity School
- **Expertise:** Theology, visual storytelling, weekly lectionary commentary
- **Notable Work:** Co-founder and creative director of the Emmy Award-winning SALT Project; former faculty at Harvard Divinity School; weekly *SALT Lectionary Commentary* used by pastors across traditions
- **Why He Fits:** Boulton and SALT represent an innovative approach to lectionary engagement through multimedia. Their weekly commentary is progressive and accessible. A conversation about how to make ancient texts vivid for modern congregations through creative media would bring a fresh angle to Fresh Text.
