# Actionable Strategies to Grow the Fresh Text Podcast Audience

## 1. SEO & DISCOVERABILITY

### Optimize Your Podcast Title with a Tagline
Podcasts that use taglines in their title and author fields achieve **6.7x better keyword rankings on Apple Podcasts** and **5.6x better on Spotify**, yet over 90% of podcasts don't use them. Currently, the show appears simply as "Fresh Text" on most platforms.

**Action:** Change the podcast title to something like:
> **Fresh Text — Weekly Lectionary Preaching Ideas for Pastors**

This front-loads critical search keywords ("lectionary," "preaching," "pastors") that your target audience is actually searching for. Ensure the same tagline appears in the author/subtitle fields on Podbean.

### Keyword-Optimize Every Episode Title
Avoid generic titles. Instead of "Mark 14:32-52 with Amanda Drury," use:
> **Mark 14:32-52: Gethsemane Sermon Ideas — with Amanda Drury**

Include the scripture reference, a thematic keyword, and the guest name. Podcasts that mention their primary keyword 5+ times in episode descriptions rank **9 positions higher** on average.

### Publish Full Transcripts
Google cannot crawl audio — it reads text. Full transcripts turn every episode into a searchable blog post. Podbean's AI tools now offer automated transcription. **Publish transcripts for every new episode and retroactively for your back catalog.** This is the single biggest SEO multiplier for podcasts.

### Build a Dedicated Podcast Website (or Enhance Your Current One)
Every episode should have its own page functioning as a search landing page with: title, summary, transcript, timestamps, scripture references, and internal links to related episodes. The IWU/Called Collective connection gives you institutional web infrastructure — use it. A page on the IWU School of Theology & Ministry site linking to every episode with proper schema markup would be powerful.

### Add Timestamps and Chapter Markers
Use Podbean's AI chapter marker tool. Timestamps with keywords (e.g., "12:30 — Sermon illustration: the sleeping disciples") improve both usability and discoverability, especially on Apple Podcasts which is expanding chapter-based features.

## 2. SOCIAL MEDIA STRATEGY

### Choose Two Platforms and Go Deep
For your audience of pastors and preachers, focus on **Instagram** and **Facebook**. Instagram Reels are the only post type shown to new people organically, and Facebook is where pastors already gather in professional groups.

### Create a Weekly Content Cadence Tied to the Lectionary
The lectionary gives you a built-in content calendar that literally never runs out:

- **Monday (Episode Drop Day):** Post an audiogram or 60-second video clip of the most provocative sermon starter idea from the episode. Caption it with the scripture reference and a hook question.
- **Tuesday:** Post a quote graphic — a single compelling sentence from the episode overlaid on a branded template. Use the scripture reference as a hashtag.
- **Wednesday:** Post a carousel (Instagram) or thread (Facebook) — "3 Sermon Angles for [Scripture Reference] This Sunday." Pull these directly from the episode.
- **Thursday:** Post a short Reel/video of John Drury giving a 30-second "If I were preaching this text, I'd..." direct-to-camera take.
- **Friday:** Share a "Weekend Prep Reminder" story linking to the episode, framed as "Still working on Sunday's sermon? We've got you."

### Hashtag Strategy
Use a consistent set: `#FreshTextPodcast` `#LectionaryPreaching` `#SermonPrep` `#RCL` `#RevisedCommonLectionary` `#SundaySermon` `#PastorLife` `#PreachingIdeas` plus the specific scripture reference each week (e.g., `#Mark14` `#Luke10`).

### Leverage Reels and Shorts
Reels on Instagram deliver the highest reach for small accounts. YouTube Shorts outperform TikTok and Instagram Reels for creators with under 5,000 followers — averaging **2,600 views per Short** vs. 660 on TikTok and 600 on Reels at the same follower level. Create 30–60 second clips of the best moment from each episode. Tools like Headliner or Podbean's built-in audiogram feature can automate this.

## 3. YOUTUBE & VIDEO STRATEGY

### Adopt the Three-Tier Content Funnel
The winning 2025-2026 formula for preaching content is: **Full episode (long-form) --> Topical clips (10-15 min) --> Shorts (under 3 min).**

- **Full Episode:** Upload the complete audio episode to YouTube with a static image or simple video. YouTube is now the #1 podcast platform (33% of weekly listeners), ahead of Spotify (26%) and Apple (14%). You are missing this audience entirely if you're audio-only.
- **Topical Clips:** Pull 10-15 minute segments focused on a single sermon idea or exegetical insight. Title these with the scripture reference and a specific preaching angle.
- **YouTube Shorts:** Create 30-60 second vertical clips of the single best sermon starter idea from each episode. Include a CTA: "Full episode in the link."

### YouTube SEO for Each Upload
- Use scripture references in titles, descriptions, and tags
- Include full transcripts in the description (or link to them)
- Add chapters/timestamps
- Create custom thumbnails with the scripture reference prominently displayed
- Tag related videos (Working Preacher, Pulpit Fiction, etc.)

### Consider a Simple Video Recording Setup
Even basic webcam footage of the conversation outperforms static audio-only uploads on YouTube. YouTube surpassed **1 billion monthly podcast viewers** and users consume over **100 million hours of podcasts daily** on the platform.

Your direct ecosystem includes podcasts that serve the same weekly lectionary cycle. These are not competitors — pastors listen to multiple sermon prep resources each week. Target:

- **Working Preacher / Sermon Brainwave** (Luther Seminary) — the biggest player, 1M+ downloads
- **Pulpit Fiction** (Rev. Eric Fistler & Rev. Robb McCoy) — RCL-focused, pop culture angle
- **Lectionary Kick-Start** (Concordia Seminary) — newer, growing fast

3. **Episode Swaps:** Offer a Fresh Text episode as bonus content for a partner podcast.
4. **Joint Special Episodes:** Co-host an episode with another lectionary podcast for a high-profile liturgical week (Easter Vigil, Advent 1, etc.).

## 5. LEVERAGING THE LECTIONARY CYCLE FOR CONTENT MARKETING

### Build an "Evergreen + Timely" Content Strategy

- **Timely (Weekly):** Each new episode serves pastors preparing for a specific upcoming Sunday. Market it with urgency: "Preaching Luke 10 this Sunday? Here are 3 sermon starters."
- **Evergreen (Cyclical):** The lectionary repeats on a 3-year cycle. Resurface old episodes when those texts come back around.

### Create a Lectionary-Indexed Episode Archive
On your website, organize episodes by scripture reference and liturgical season. A pastor searching "sermon ideas for Proper 15 Year C" should land on your episode page.

### Seasonal Marketing Pushes
- **Advent (November-December):** Pastors plan sermon series; push Advent-related episodes hard
- **Lent (February-March):** Same dynamic
- **Holy Week / Easter:** Peak sermon anxiety = peak search traffic
- **Ordinary Time (post-Pentecost):** Market as "your companion through the long green season"
