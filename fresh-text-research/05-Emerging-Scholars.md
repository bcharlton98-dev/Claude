## Recommended Emerging Scholar Guests for the Fresh Text Podcast

### 1. Dr. Matt O'Reilly
- **Institution:** Wesley Biblical Seminary (Director of Research); Lead Pastor, Christ Church Birmingham (Global Methodist Church)
- **Expertise:** New Testament studies (Paul, resurrection, sanctification); Wesleyan biblical theology; preaching
- **Why he fits:** O'Reilly is a pastor-scholar in the truest sense -- he holds a PhD in NT from the University of Gloucestershire, pastors a Global Methodist congregation, and publishes with Seedbed. His book *Free to Be Holy: A Biblical Theology of Sanctification* directly connects NT exegesis with Wesleyan theology. He is a two-time John Stott Award recipient and a Senior Fellow of the Center for Pastor Theologians. He would bring both exegetical depth and pastoral application to lectionary passages.

### 2. Dr. Murray Vasser
- **Institution:** Wesley Biblical Seminary, Assistant Professor of New Testament
- **Expertise:** Pauline epistles (Ephesians, Colossians household codes); biblical inerrancy in Wesleyan context
- **Why he fits:** Vasser is genuinely early-career -- he completed his PhD at Asbury Theological Seminary under Ben Witherington III and Craig Keener in 2021 and joined WBS faculty in 2022-23. His unusual background (aerospace engineer turned NT scholar) gives him a distinctive voice. He writes for *WORLD* and *Christianity Today* on Wesleyan biblical interpretation and would bring fresh energy to lectionary texts, especially Pauline epistles.

### 3. Dr. Suzanne Nicholson
- **Institution:** Asbury University, Professor of New Testament
- **Expertise:** Pauline theology (*Dynamic Oneness*); women in the NT; Wesleyan Scripture interpretation; the Asbury Outpouring
- **Why she fits:** Nicholson is an elder in the Global Methodist Church, Assistant Lead Editor of Firebrand Magazine, and a Seedbed author. She co-authored the "Next Methodism" document signed by 64 Wesleyan scholars. Her recent work on AI, spiritual formation, and the means of grace (presented at the 2026 WTS meeting) shows she is engaging cutting-edge questions from a Wesleyan perspective. She would be especially strong on epistles and the intersection of Scripture and formation.

### 4. Dr. Carla Works
- **Institution:** Wesley Theological Seminary, Professor of New Testament (recently elevated to leadership role)
- **Expertise:** Pauline epistles; NT use of Israel's scriptures; biblical ethics; theological interpretation
- **Why she fits:** Works holds a PhD from Princeton Theological Seminary and is a contributor to Working Preacher -- meaning she already writes lectionary-facing biblical scholarship. Her book *The Least of These: Paul and the Marginalized* (Eerdmans, 2020) would resonate with the podcast's pastor audience. She is at a United Methodist seminary and bridges the academic-pastoral divide naturally.

### 5. Dr. Jamal-Dominique Hopkins
- **Institution:** Baylor University / Truett Theological Seminary, Associate Professor of Christian Scriptures & Director of Black Church Studies
- **Expertise:** Biblical studies; Late Second Temple Judaism; Dead Sea Scrolls; AME tradition
- **Why he fits:** Hopkins is the only known scholar of African descent specializing in the Dead Sea Scrolls. He is an ordained AME elder, previously founded Dickerson-Green Theological Seminary at Allen University (AME), and holds a Yale Pedagogy Fellowship. He would bring a unique perspective combining AME Methodist roots, rigorous biblical scholarship, and cross-cultural experience to lectionary discussions.

### 6. Dr. Ryan Danker
- **Institution:** Director, John Wesley Institute; formerly Wesley Theological Seminary and Greensboro College faculty
- **Expertise:** Wesleyan history and theology; the Evangelical Revival; Methodist political theology
- **Why he fits:** Danker holds degrees from Northwest Nazarene, Duke, and Boston University. His *Wesley and the Anglicans* (IVP Academic, 2016) established him as a rising Wesley scholar. He leads the School of Methodism and Epworth Fellows programs that connect Wesleyan scholarship to laypeople and young adults. As a historian who engages Scripture through the Wesleyan lens, he could provide rich historical-theological context for lectionary passages, especially during seasons tied to Methodist heritage.

### 7. Dr. Brad E. Kelle
- **Institution:** Point Loma Nazarene University, Professor of Old Testament and Hebrew
- **Expertise:** OT prophets (especially Hosea and Ezekiel); warfare in ancient Israel; trauma studies; Wesleyan OT interpretation
- **Why he fits:** Kelle is one of the foremost Wesleyan Old Testament scholars working today, authoring the forthcoming Oxford Handbook of Hosea and a Hosea commentary for the Old Testament Library series. Critically, he writes in explicit dialogue with Wesleyan theology -- his *Ezekiel* commentary is in the New Beacon Bible Commentary (Wesleyan tradition) series. He would be an excellent guest for OT lectionary weeks, bringing Wesleyan prophetic theology to bear on the text.

### 8. Dr. Kevin M. Watson
- **Institution:** Asbury Theological Seminary (Director of Academic Growth and Formation); formerly tenured at Candler School of Theology, Emory University
- **Expertise:** Wesleyan history; doctrine of entire sanctification; small-group formation; Methodist ecclesiology
- **Why he fits:** Watson's *Doctrine, Spirit, and Discipline: A History of the Wesleyan Tradition in the United States* (Zondervan, 2024) is already becoming a standard seminary textbook. He won the 2020 WTS Smith/Wynkoop Book Award. As someone recently tenured at Candler who then moved to Asbury, he is at a pivotal career moment. His deep knowledge of Wesley's theology would enrich lectionary conversations with historical-theological depth, especially around sanctification and spiritual formation themes in Scripture.

### 9. Dr. Courtney V. Buggs
- **Institution:** Christian Theological Seminary, Director of PhD Program in African American Preaching and Sacred Rhetoric; Assistant Professor of Homiletics
- **Expertise:** Homiletics; ecumenical preaching; women and preaching; womanist theology
- **Why she fits:** Buggs (PhD, Emory; MDiv, Candler) succeeded Frank A. Thomas to lead the world's only PhD program in African American Preaching and Sacred Rhetoric. Her experience spans Pentecostal, Methodist, and Baptist traditions, and she brings a rare combination of military leadership (retired Lt. Colonel, USAF) and homiletical scholarship. She would offer the podcast a distinctive voice on the craft of preaching itself -- how to move from lectionary text to sermon.

### 10. Rev. Dr. Neichelle R. Guidry
- **Institution:** Spelman College, Dean of Sisters Chapel and Director of the WISDOM Center
- **Expertise:** Womanist homiletics; liturgical studies; preaching and young adults; African American women in ministry
- **Why she fits:** Guidry (PhD, Garrett-Evangelical) was named one of TIME Magazine's "12 New Faces of Black Leadership" and one of the "Top Five Young Preachers in America" by ROHO. She trained under Otis Moss III at Trinity UCC in Chicago and created shepreaches, a professional development organization for Black women in ministry. Formerly at Garrett-Evangelical (a Methodist seminary), she brings homiletical expertise and a younger generational voice that could resonate with the podcast's goal of equipping the next generation of preachers.

### 11. Dr. Michael D. Jackson
- **Institution:** Trevecca Nazarene University, Professor of Homiletics and Hebrew Bible
- **Expertise:** Preaching; Hebrew Bible; pastoral ministry in the Church of the Nazarene
- **Why he fits:** Jackson leads the $1.25 million Lilly Endowment-funded "More and Better Preachers" initiative at Trevecca, focused on recruiting and equipping preachers in the Nazarene tradition. He hosts the "More and Better Preachers" podcast, making him a natural peer-podcaster. His dual appointment in homiletics and Hebrew Bible -- the exact intersection of preaching and biblical studies -- makes him ideal for discussing OT lectionary passages from a Wesleyan-holiness perspective.

### 12. Dr. Amy Peeler
- **Institution:** Wheaton College (Kenneth T. Wessner Chair of Biblical Studies); Associate Rector, St. Mark's Episcopal Church
- **Expertise:** Epistle to the Hebrews; theological interpretation of Scripture; Mary in NT theology; feminist theology
- **Why she fits:** Peeler (PhD, Princeton Theological Seminary) is a Fellow with the Center for Pastor Theologians and actively preaches monthly at her parish, embodying the pastor-scholar model. Her Hebrews commentary (Eerdmans, Commentaries for Christian Formation series) is explicitly designed to support preaching and Bible teaching. She is also associate editor of the New International Commentary on the New Testament. When Hebrews passages come up in the lectionary, she would be an exceptional guest.

### 13. Dr. David F. Watson
- **Institution:** Incoming President, Asbury Theological Seminary (as of July 2025); formerly Academic Dean and Professor of New Testament at United Theological Seminary
- **Expertise:** Gospel of Mark (honor/shame); Wesleyan biblical interpretation; Scripture and the life of God; disability ministry
- **Why he fits:** Watson (PhD, Southern Methodist University) is the founding lead editor of Firebrand Magazine and a Global Methodist elder. His book *Scripture and the Life of God* (Seedbed) directly addresses how Scripture functions in the life of faith -- a natural topic for lectionary-based preaching. As he transitions to leading Asbury Seminary, he is at a unique inflection point where he is shaping the future of Wesleyan theological education. He would be a strong guest for Gospel of Mark lectionary weeks in particular.

### 14. Dr. Rachel Coleman
- **Institution:** Teaches at Indiana Wesleyan University, United Theological Seminary, and Asbury Theological Seminary; One Mission Society theological education consultant
- **Expertise:** New Testament; global/cross-cultural theological education; Latin American ministry
- **Why she fits:** Coleman (PhD, Regent University) already has a direct connection to IWU and sits on the Firebrand editorial board. Her cross-cultural perspective from years of missionary work in Ecuador and ongoing Latin American teaching would bring a global dimension to lectionary discussions. As someone who teaches at multiple Wesleyan institutions simultaneously, she is deeply embedded in the tradition.

### 15. Steve Hoskins
- **Institution:** Trevecca Nazarene University, Associate Professor of Religion
- **Expertise:** Wesleyan historical theology; Charles Wesley; Wesleyan Theological Society leadership
- **Why he fits:** Hoskins serves as Promotional Secretary of the Wesleyan Theological Society, editor for the Charles Wesley Society, and founder of the Wesleyan Historical Society. He sits on the Firebrand editorial board. His deep knowledge of Charles Wesley's hymns and theology could bring a unique liturgical-poetic dimension to lectionary conversations, especially during Advent, Lent, and other seasons where Wesley's hymns intersect with lectionary texts.
