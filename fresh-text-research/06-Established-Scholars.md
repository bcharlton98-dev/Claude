# Recommended Guests for the Fresh Text Podcast

Below are 20 established scholars who would be excellent fits for the podcast, organized by area of strength. Each person brings scholarly depth combined with an ability to communicate accessibly to pastors and preachers.

---

## Old Testament / Hebrew Bible Scholars

### 1. Ellen F. Davis
- **Institution:** Duke Divinity School (Amos Ragan Kearns Distinguished Professor of Bible and Practical Theology)
- **Expertise:** Old Testament, preaching the Hebrew Bible, ecological theology, interfaith dialogue
- **Notable Publications:** *Wondrous Depth: Preaching the Old Testament* (WJK, 2005); *Opening Israel's Scriptures* (Oxford, 2019); *Scripture, Culture, and Agriculture* (Cambridge, 2009); *Preaching the Luminous Word* (Eerdmans, 2016)
- **Why she fits:** Davis is arguably the premier scholar working at the exact intersection of Old Testament and preaching. Her guiding question -- "So what? If we read the text this way, who cares?" -- is precisely the posture Fresh Text takes. She has taught at Duke, Yale, and Virginia Theological Seminary, and is known for being an extraordinary communicator who makes Hebrew Bible scholarship come alive for pastors.

### 2. Bill T. Arnold
- **Institution:** Asbury Theological Seminary (Paul S. Amos Professor of Old Testament Interpretation)
- **Expertise:** Pentateuch, Genesis, Deuteronomy, ancient Near Eastern studies, Hebrew language
- **Notable Publications:** *The Cambridge Companion to Genesis* (ed., 2022); *Encountering the Old Testament*; Genesis and 1-2 Samuel commentaries; "Genesis" in the *Wesley One Volume Commentary* (Abingdon, 2020); editor of OT notes in *The Wesley Study Bible*
- **Why he fits:** Arnold is deeply embedded in the Wesleyan tradition at Asbury Seminary and has explicitly dedicated his career to empowering Wesleyan churches through biblical scholarship. He co-translated Genesis for the Common English Bible and has published in the *Wesleyan Theological Journal*. A natural fit for a Wesleyan lectionary podcast.

### 3. Sandra L. Richter
- **Institution:** Westmont College (Robert H. Gundry Chair of Biblical Studies); formerly at Asbury Seminary and Wesley Biblical Seminary
- **Expertise:** Deuteronomy, Deuteronomistic History, environmental theology, biblical theology for the church
- **Notable Publications:** *The Epic of Eden: A Christian Entry into the Old Testament* (IVP, 2008); DVD curricula on Isaiah, Ruth, and Jonah with Seedbed; forthcoming commentary on Deuteronomy (Eerdmans)
- **Why she fits:** Richter has taught at both Asbury Seminary and Wesley Biblical Seminary, giving her deep Wesleyan roots. Her *Epic of Eden* materials are widely used in churches and demonstrate her gift for making Old Testament scholarship accessible. She is a bridge-builder between academy and church.

### 4. Matthew Schlimm
- **Institution:** University of Dubuque Theological Seminary (Professor of Old Testament)
- **Expertise:** Old Testament ethics, Genesis, Torah, Hebrew language for pastors, Wesleyan theology
- **Notable Publications:** *This Strange and Sacred Scripture*; *70 Hebrew Words Every Christian Should Know*; *From Fratricide to Forgiveness* (Eisenbrauns, 2011); associate editor of the *Common English Bible Study Bible*; articles in the *Wesleyan Theological Journal*
- **Why he fits:** Schlimm is an ordained United Methodist elder, a John Wesley Fellow, and a member of the Wesleyan Theological Society. He writes for Working Preacher and has published specifically on Wesley's engagement with biblical criticism. His book on Hebrew words for Christians is exactly the kind of accessible scholarship that Fresh Text listeners would value.

## New Testament Scholars

### 5. Craig Keener
- **Institution:** Asbury Theological Seminary (F.M. and Ada Thompson Professor of Biblical Studies)
- **Expertise:** Acts, miracles in the New Testament, historical Jesus, Gospels, Paul
- **Notable Publications:** Massive commentaries on Acts (4 vols.), Matthew, John, Romans, Galatians, and 1-2 Corinthians; multiple Christianity Today Book Awards; *The IVP Bible Background Commentary: New Testament*
- **Why he fits:** Keener is one of the most prolific New Testament scholars alive and is at the flagship Wesleyan seminary. His background commentary provides exactly the kind of contextual insight that makes lectionary preaching richer. His range across the entire New Testament means he could speak to virtually any lectionary text.

### 6. Ben Witherington III
- **Institution:** Asbury Theological Seminary (Jean R. Amos Professor of New Testament)
- **Expertise:** Historical Jesus, Pauline studies, socio-rhetorical criticism, all NT books
- **Notable Publications:** Over 60 books including commentaries on every NT book; *The Jesus Quest* (Christianity Today Book of the Year, 1995); widely read blog "The Bible and Culture"
- **Why he fits:** Witherington is a towering figure in Wesleyan New Testament scholarship and a gifted communicator who runs a popular blog reaching pastors and laypeople. He has been at Asbury since 1995 and embodies the pastor-scholar ideal that Fresh Text celebrates.

### 7. Nijay Gupta
- **Institution:** Northern Seminary (Julius R. Mantey Professor of New Testament)
- **Expertise:** Pauline theology, Philippians, Colossians, Thessalonians, worship in Paul
- **Notable Publications:** *Strange Religion* (Brazos, 2024); *Tell Her Story* (IVP Academic, 2023); *Paul and the Language of Faith*; co-editor of *Dictionary of Paul and His Letters* (2nd ed.); commentaries on Galatians, Philippians, Colossians, 1-2 Thessalonians
- **Why he fits:** Gupta is a rising star who combines serious Pauline scholarship with accessibility. He co-chairs the Pauline Theology seminar of the Institute for Biblical Research, serves as a senior NLT translator, and has written on women's leadership in the early church -- all topics that would resonate with Fresh Text's audience.

### 8. Amy Peeler
- **Institution:** Wheaton College (Kenneth T. Wessner Professor of New Testament); Associate Priest at St. Mark's Episcopal Church
- **Expertise:** Epistle to the Hebrews, feminist theology, Christology, atonement
- **Notable Publications:** *Women and the Gender of God* (Eerdmans, 2022); *Hebrews* commentary (Commentaries for Christian Formation); *"You Are My Son"* (T&T Clark, 2014); associate editor, New International Commentary on the New Testament
- **Why she fits:** Peeler did postdoctoral work at Indiana Wesleyan University (Fresh Text's home institution), so there is a direct personal connection. She is both a scholar and a practicing priest, embodying the pastor-scholar model. Her work on Hebrews is directly relevant to lectionary preaching, and her book on women and the gender of God has generated important conversations in the church.

### 9. Esau McCaulley
- **Institution:** Wheaton College (Jonathan Blanchard Associate Professor of New Testament and Public Theology)
- **Expertise:** Pauline theology, African American biblical interpretation, public theology
- **Notable Publications:** *Reading While Black* (IVP, 2020; Christianity Today Book of the Year); *How Far to the Promised Land* (memoir, 2023); editor of *New Testament in Color*; contributing opinion writer for the New York Times
- **Why he fits:** McCaulley brings a distinctive voice as a Black evangelical New Testament scholar who reads Scripture through the lens of the Black church tradition. His "hermeneutic of trust" -- wrestling honestly with hard texts while maintaining confidence in God's character -- aligns well with the Wesleyan commitment to Scripture. He would bring a perspective Fresh Text's audience needs to hear.

### 10. Jaime Clark-Soles
- **Institution:** Perkins School of Theology, SMU (Professor of New Testament; Altshuler Distinguished Teaching Professor)
- **Expertise:** Johannine literature (Gospel of John, Revelation), death and afterlife in the NT, disability and the Bible, NT ethics
- **Notable Publications:** *1 Corinthians: Searching the Depths of God* (2021); *Women in the Bible* (2020); *Reading John for Dear Life* (2016); *Death and Afterlife in the New Testament* (T&T Clark, 2006)
- **Why she fits:** Clark-Soles is at a Methodist seminary (Perkins/SMU) and has won distinguished teaching awards, demonstrating her ability to communicate complex ideas accessibly. She regularly teaches laypeople through church lectures and study trips, and her work on John's Gospel is directly relevant to lectionary preaching. Her expertise in 1 Corinthians and the Johannine writings covers texts that appear frequently in the RCL.

### 11. Emerson B. Powery
- **Institution:** Messiah University (Dean, School of Arts, Culture & Society; Professor of Biblical Studies)
- **Expertise:** New Testament and Christian origins, African American biblical interpretation, Gospel of Matthew, the Bible and slavery
- **Notable Publications:** *The Good Samaritan: Luke 10 for the Life of the Church* (Baker, 2023); co-author of *The Genesis of Liberation* (WJK, 2016); *Jesus Reads Scripture* (Brill, 2003); co-editor of *True to Our Native Land: An African American NT Commentary* (Fortress, 2007)
- **Why he fits:** Powery (PhD, Duke) writes for Working Preacher and has served on the editorial board of the *Journal of Biblical Literature*. His recent book on the Good Samaritan parable demonstrates his interest in bringing scholarship to bear on preaching well-known texts. He brings an important African American interpretive perspective.

### 12. Carla Works
- **Institution:** Wesley Theological Seminary (Professor of New Testament)
- **Expertise:** Pauline epistles, NT use of Israel's Scriptures, biblical ethics, theological interpretation
- **Notable Publications:** *The Church in the Wilderness: Paul's Use of Exodus Traditions in 1 Corinthians* (Mohr Siebeck, 2014); *The Least of These: Paul and the Marginalized* (Eerdmans, 2020); "Philippians" in *The Women's Bible Commentary*
- **Why she fits:** Works is at a United Methodist seminary, holds degrees from Yale Divinity and Princeton Seminary, and works on Pauline texts that appear constantly in the lectionary. Her focus on how Paul engages marginalized communities offers a fresh angle for preaching.

## Homiletics / Preaching Scholars

### 13. Luke A. Powery
- **Institution:** Duke University Chapel (Dean) and Duke Divinity School (Professor of Homiletics and African and African American Studies)
- **Expertise:** Preaching, pneumatology, music and culture in the African diaspora, lectionary commentary
- **Notable Publications:** *Becoming Human: The Holy Spirit and the Rhetoric of Race* (2023 Book of the Year, Religious Communication Association); *Dem Dry Bones: Preaching, Death, and Hope*; *Rise Up, Shepherd! Advent Reflections on the Spirituals*; General Editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary series
- **Why he fits:** As a general editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary -- the most significant recent lectionary resource -- Powery is at the epicenter of lectionary preaching scholarship. He is an ordained minister who preaches regularly at Duke Chapel and was named one of the most effective preachers in the English-speaking world by Baylor. His Advent and Lenten books on the spirituals offer rich sermon material.

### 14. Carolyn J. Sharp
- **Institution:** Yale Divinity School (Professor of Homiletics; formerly Professor of Hebrew Scriptures)
- **Expertise:** Hebrew prophets, homiletics, ecotheology, biblical poetics, feminist perspectives
- **Notable Publications:** Commentaries on Micah (2024), Jeremiah 26-52 (2022), Joshua (2019); *The Prophetic Literature* (2019); *Irony and Meaning in the Hebrew Bible* (2009); General Editor of *Connections* lectionary commentary series
- **Why she fits:** Sharp is the rare scholar tenured in two different disciplines (Hebrew Bible, then homiletics) at Yale. She bridges Old Testament scholarship and preaching theory in a way that is directly relevant to Fresh Text's mission. As a co-editor of the *Connections* lectionary series, she is deeply invested in helping pastors preach from the lectionary.

### 15. Valerie Bridgeman
- **Institution:** Methodist Theological School in Ohio (Dean and VP for Academic Affairs; Associate Professor of Homiletics and Hebrew Bible)
- **Expertise:** Hebrew Bible, homiletics, womanist interpretation, prophetic preaching
- **Notable Publications:** Entry on Homiletics in *The Oxford Encyclopedia of Biblical Interpretation* (2013); commentary on Hosea (co-authored with Cheryl Kirk-Duggan); commentary on Job (Wisdom Commentary series); co-editor of volume on womanist methodologies (Fortress Press)
- **Why she fits:** Bridgeman is at a Methodist seminary and uniquely holds expertise in both Hebrew Bible and homiletics -- exactly the combination Fresh Text needs. She is the founding president of WomanPreach! Inc. and was inducted into the Martin Luther King Jr. Collegium of Scholars and Preachers at Morehouse College. She writes for Working Preacher.

### 16. Joy J. Moore
- **Institution:** Northern Seminary (Professor of Homiletics); formerly at Luther Seminary, Asbury Seminary, Duke Divinity, and Fuller Seminary
- **Expertise:** Biblical preaching, practical theology, African American church studies
- **Notable Publications:** Contributor to Sojourners, Christian Century, Working Preacher; co-host of Luther Seminary's "Sermon Brainwave" podcast (2019-24); past president of the Wesleyan Theological Society (2021-22)
- **Why she fits:** Moore is an ordained United Methodist elder and former president of the Wesleyan Theological Society. She co-hosted the "Sermon Brainwave" podcast at Luther Seminary, which is a near-identical format to Fresh Text (scholars discussing lectionary texts for preaching). Her career spanning Asbury, Duke, Fuller, and Luther gives her a remarkable breadth of perspective. She earned her MDiv from Garrett-Evangelical, another Methodist seminary.

## Wesleyan/Methodist Theologians and Biblical Scholars

### 17. Suzanne Nicholson
- **Institution:** Asbury University (Professor of New Testament)
- **Expertise:** Pauline theology, Ephesians, inductive Bible study, Wesleyan-Holiness theology
- **Notable Publications:** *Dynamic Oneness: The Significance and Flexibility of Paul's One-God Language*; Ephesians chapter in the *Wesley One Volume Commentary*; *Cooperating with the Holy Spirit* (on the Asbury Outpouring); Women in the New Testament Bible study (Seedbed); commentary on John 1-12 for the New Beacon Bible Commentary
- **Why she fits:** Nicholson is deeply embedded in the Wesleyan-Holiness world -- she is an elder in the Global Methodist Church, serves on its Commission for Doctrine, and writes for Seedbed and Firebrand. She is at Asbury University (sister institution to Asbury Seminary, both in Wilmore, KY). Her writing for the New Beacon Bible Commentary and the Wesley One Volume Commentary shows her commitment to serving the Wesleyan church.

### 18. Laura C. Sweat Holmes
- **Institution:** Wesley Theological Seminary (Professor of New Testament)
- **Expertise:** Gospel of Mark, Gospel of John, theology of the Gospels, paradox in biblical narrative
- **Notable Publications:** *The Theological Role of Paradox in the Gospel of Mark* (Bloomsbury, 2013); commentary on John 1-12 (New Beacon Bible Commentary Series)
- **Why she fits:** Holmes is a lifelong United Methodist at a Methodist seminary who describes herself as "called to and fulfilled by teaching Scripture to and for the church." Her work on the Gospels -- especially Mark and John, which feature prominently in the lectionary -- makes her an ideal conversation partner. Her New Beacon Bible Commentary work connects her to the Wesleyan publishing world.

## Bridge Builders: Academy and Church

### 19. Thomas G. Long
- **Institution:** Candler School of Theology, Emory University (Bandy Professor Emeritus of Preaching)
- **Expertise:** Homiletics theory and practice, biblical preaching, liturgy, funeral preaching
- **Notable Publications:** *The Witness of Preaching* (named one of 25 most influential preaching books in 25 years); *Preaching from Memory to Hope* (Lyman Beecher Lectures at Yale); commentaries on Hebrews, Matthew, and the Pastoral Epistles; co-editor of *Connections* lectionary commentary
- **Why he fits:** Long was named one of the twelve most effective preachers in the English-speaking world by Baylor University. He is a co-editor of the *Connections* lectionary commentary and served as senior homiletics editor of the New Interpreter's Bible. Though Presbyterian, he taught at the Methodist-affiliated Candler School for 15 years and has deep ecumenical sensibilities. His emeritus status means he likely has time for podcast conversations.

### 20. David R. Bauer
- **Institution:** Asbury Theological Seminary (Ralph W. Beeson Professor of Inductive Biblical Studies; Dean of the School of Biblical Interpretation)
- **Expertise:** Inductive Bible study, Gospel of Matthew, Hebrews, hermeneutics
- **Notable Publications:** *Inductive Bible Study* (Baker Academic, with Robert Traina); recipient of the American Bible Society Award for Excellence in Biblical Studies
- **Why he fits:** Bauer has been at Asbury since 1984 and is the foremost living practitioner of inductive Bible study, which originated in the Wesleyan tradition. His methodology -- careful, text-centered observation before interpretation -- aligns perfectly with Fresh Text's approach of reading the text carefully before developing sermon ideas. He is an ordained Free Methodist elder, placing him squarely in the Wesleyan family.

## Notes on Fit with Fresh Text

Based on my research into the podcast's format (reading a passage, scholarly-but-accessible conversation, sermon starter ideas), the strongest fits are scholars who:

- **Already work at the academy-church intersection**: Davis, Arnold, Richter, Schlimm, Peeler, McCaulley, Powery (Luke), Sharp, Bridgeman, and Long all have track records of writing or speaking for pastors, not just academics.
- **Have Wesleyan/Methodist connections**: Arnold, Witherington, Keener, Schlimm, Moore, Nicholson, Bauer, Holmes, and Works are all either at Wesleyan institutions or ordained in Wesleyan/Methodist traditions.
- **Contribute to lectionary resources**: Luke Powery, Carolyn Sharp, and Thomas Long co-edit the *Connections* lectionary commentary. Schlimm, Bridgeman, Clark-Soles, and Emerson Powery write for Working Preacher. These scholars are already thinking about lectionary texts week by week.
- **Bring diverse perspectives**: McCaulley, Luke Powery, Bridgeman, Moore, Emerson Powery, and Valerie Bridgeman bring African American interpretive traditions. Peeler, Clark-Soles, Works, Sharp, Davis, Holmes, Nicholson, and Richter are women scholars in fields still dominated by men.
