# Podcasting & Enrollment at Seminaries, Divinity Schools, Bible Colleges, and Christian Universities

## Research Brief — March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Despite the growing prevalence of podcasts produced by theological faculty and faith-based institutions, **there is no publicly documented case in which a podcast has been directly and causally linked to measurable enrollment increases** at a seminary, divinity school, Bible college, or Christian university. However, substantial circumstantial and anecdotal evidence suggests that high-profile faculty podcasts elevate institutional brand awareness, and one peer-reviewed study demonstrates that podcast advertising positively influences prospective students' attitudes toward universities. The findings below are organized by strength of evidence.

---

## I. STRONG / DOCUMENTED EVIDENCE

### Podcast Advertising Influences Prospective Students (Peer-Reviewed)

A study published in the *Atlantic Marketing Journal* by Cistulli and Snyder (Central Connecticut State University) measured the impact of a graduate program advertisement embedded mid-roll in a podcast episode. Key findings:

- **Ads produced positive effects** on attitude toward the ad, attitude toward the university (brand), and behavioral intentions to consider attending.
- **Attitude toward the podcast hosts influenced** both attitude toward the ad and attitude toward the university — listeners who already consumed podcasts reported even stronger positive attitudes.
- The authors conclude: "Universities — or any organization — would benefit from understanding the podcast listening habits of their specific target markets to make sure ads are placed in the appropriate podcasts."
- Sample: 226 U.S. participants aged 18–25 with undergraduate degrees; 84% reported listening to audio podcasts.

**Implication:** While this study examines *advertising within* podcasts rather than *hosting* a podcast, it establishes that the podcast medium carries measurable persuasive power for higher education marketing.

> Source: Cistulli, M.D. & Snyder, J.L., "Podcast Advertising and Higher Education," *Atlantic Marketing Journal*, Vol. 14, Iss. 2. ([Full text](https://digitalcommons.kennesaw.edu/amj/vol14/iss2/6/))

### Wiley Higher Education: Podcasting as Recruitment Tool

A Wiley University Services report notes that institutions can use podcasts to "provide access to academic resources through mobile devices" and that "this medium also offers student recruitment potential by building brand awareness with a broad population of prospective students."

> Source: [Wiley — Marketing to Potential Higher Education Students With Podcasting](https://universityservices.wiley.com/the-potential-of-podcasting-in-higher-education-marketing/)

## II. ANECDOTAL / STRONGLY SUGGESTIVE EVIDENCE

### Kate Bowler — Duke Divinity School ("Everything Happens") — DEEP DIVE

Kate Bowler, Associate Professor of American Religious History at Duke Divinity, hosts *Everything Happens with Kate Bowler* (produced by Lemonada Media). This is the strongest circumstantial case in theological education for a faculty podcast elevating institutional visibility.

#### The Platform by the Numbers

- **18+ million podcast downloads** across 13 seasons (launched January 2018)
- **~9 million views** on her TED Talk
- **4x *New York Times* bestselling author** (first memoir sold 200,000+ copies)
- **270+ podcast episodes** produced over 7 years
- Reached the **iTunes top 100**
- Featured in *The New York Times*, *The Washington Post*, NPR (*Wild Card with Rachel Martin*, Jan 2025; *Morning Edition*; Terry Gross), BBC, TIME, *The Atlantic*, *The Guardian*, *The Economist*, Good Morning America, the TODAY Show, and more
- Received the **2022 Distinction in Theological Education Award from Yale Divinity School**
- Two honorary doctorates (Macalester College, 2022; University of Manitoba, 2025)

*Duke Magazine* described her as "more famous than anyone on the Duke campus outside of Cameron Indoor." Colleague Will Willimon (retired United Methodist bishop, Duke Divinity faculty) told the *Washington Post*: **"Everywhere I go, people say to me, 'Do you know Kate Bowler?'"** — and acknowledged that "some faculty members probably feel some envy." ([Washington Post, Nov. 2021](https://www.washingtonpost.com/religion/2021/11/05/kate-bowler-self-help/))

Duke University selected Bowler as a **#Duke100 Centennial trailblazer**, profiling her at [100.duke.edu/story/kate-bowler](https://100.duke.edu/story/kate-bowler/).

#### The Everything Happens Initiative — Institutional Infrastructure

This is not a side project. The Everything Happens Initiative is a **formally staffed, grant-funded operation housed within Duke Divinity School**:

- **Team of 8** (4 full-time, 4 part-time) with offices on Duke's east campus, 1.5 miles from the Divinity School
- Products include: the podcast, weekly Instagram blessings, a newsletter, devotionals, and short videos
- **Dedicated Digital Marketer position**: Duke Divinity's official Administration & Faculty directory lists **Hailie Durrett, Digital Marketer, Everything Happens Initiative** as part of the school's staff — confirming the initiative is institutionally embedded, not a personal brand
- **Grant-funded through major foundations:**
  - Lilly Endowment (2018–2020): "Everything Happens Project Planning"
  - Duke Endowment (2020–2021): "Equipping Pastors for the Digital Church — Everything Happens Project"
  - Duke Endowment (2020–2023): Public service grant
  - Lilly Endowment (2020–2026): "Leadership Education Operations"
  - John Templeton Foundation (2026–2027): "The Everything Happens Project"

Bowler describes the integration: **"I've structured it so that every new giant history book I'm writing is feeding into these podcasts."** Her course titles mirror the podcast themes (e.g., "Everything Happens: Themes in American Religion").

#### Connection to Admissions & Recruitment

- Duke Divinity launched **"Divcast"** in September 2020, an admissions office podcast hosted by **Rev. Todd Maberry** (Senior Director of Admissions, Recruitment, and Student Finance). Divcast features faculty conversations, alumni interviews, and student stories across multiple series.
- A.J. Walton, Senior Director of Cross-Sector Initiatives at Duke Divinity, appeared on Divcast — his role explicitly includes **working with Kate Bowler and Everything Happens** alongside storytelling and outreach functions.
- Duke Divinity also employs a **Director of Digital Strategy, Communications** (Frith Gowan), suggesting a broader institutional commitment to digital content for recruitment.

#### Enrollment Trajectory During the Podcast Era

| Year | Metric | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| 2017 | 543 FTE | Wikipedia / ATS |
| 2018 (entering class) | 112 new MDiv + 24 MTS + 14 MACP + 9 ThM + 22 DMin + 4 ThD | Duke Divinity news |
| 2023–24 | 570 FTE / 624 headcount | ATS data |
| 2023–24 (entering class) | 215 new students (104 MDiv, 29 MTS, 22 DMin, 13 MACP) | ATS / Duke |
| 2024–25 | 599 FTE (592.20) / 813 headcount | ATS current listing |

This represents growth from **543 FTE (2017) to 599 FTE (2024–25)** — approximately **10% growth** — during a period when mainline seminary enrollment broadly declined. Duke Divinity maintains **98% of students at full course load** (highest among ATS schools) and remains the **only mainline denominational seminary in the ATS top 25** by enrollment.

#### Assessment

**No public statement from Duke directly credits the podcast with enrollment gains.** However, the weight of circumstantial evidence is substantial:

1. Duke has **institutionally embedded** the Everything Happens Initiative with dedicated staff, a digital marketer, and grant infrastructure — this is not a faculty side hustle but a school-resourced operation.
2. Bowler's platform reaches **audiences far beyond traditional seminary prospects** — her listeners are people navigating grief, illness, and meaning-making, many of whom would never otherwise encounter "Duke Divinity School."
3. Duke's enrollment **grew against the mainline trend** during the same period the podcast was scaling.
4. Senior Duke administrators' roles **explicitly bridge** between the Everything Happens platform and admissions/outreach.
5. Duke selected Bowler as one of its centennial trailblazers — signaling that the university itself views her public platform as central to its identity and brand.

The most likely reality is that Bowler's podcast functions as a **massive top-of-funnel brand awareness engine** that benefits Duke Divinity in ways the school has not yet publicly quantified — but has clearly invested in sustaining.

### Scot McKnight — Northern Seminary

Scot McKnight (Julius R. Mantey Chair of New Testament) maintained a significant public platform through his blog *Jesus Creed*, his *Kingdom Roots Podcast*, and frequent podcast guest appearances while at Northern Seminary (2013–2024). His departure — and the institutional disruption that prompted it — may offer a natural experiment: if Northern's enrollment or inquiries decline post-McKnight, it would suggest his platform had been a draw. **Worth monitoring.**

### Tim Mackie / BibleProject — Western Seminary

Tim Mackie, co-founder of BibleProject (3.5+ million YouTube subscribers), serves as an adjunct professor at Western Seminary in Portland, where he earned his Th.M. BibleProject is described as "one of the most successful Christian educational platforms worldwide."

**Assessment:** No public data links BibleProject to Western Seminary enrollment. However, the sheer scale of BibleProject's audience (millions of monthly viewers) and Mackie's ongoing adjunct role create an implicit association. Western Seminary's own website features a "[Transformation Story](https://www.westernseminary.edu/transformation-stories/tim-mackie-story)" about Mackie, suggesting the institution recognizes his brand value.

### Ed Stetzer — Biola University / Talbot School of Theology

Ed Stetzer, Dean and Professor at Talbot School of Theology (Biola University), hosts *Ed Stetzer Live* on Moody Radio. Stetzer is one of the most widely recognized voices in evangelical leadership and church research.

**Assessment:** No public data links his media presence to Biola/Talbot enrollment specifically, but his appointment as Dean itself signals that institutions value public-platform faculty for visibility and credibility.

## III. NO DIRECT EVIDENCE FOUND — BUT WORTH NOTING

### Preston Sprinkle — Eternity Bible College / "Theology in the Raw"

Preston Sprinkle's *Theology in the Raw* podcast has a large and engaged following. He served as Associate Professor and then Vice President at Eternity Bible College (2009–2016), including leading the Boise extension campus.

- **The Boise extension site failed due to low enrollment** — Sprinkle wrote publicly that "there simply weren't enough [students] to justify the extension site" and enrollment was "on a downward trajectory."
- Sprinkle departed EBC in 2016 and now leads The Center for Faith, Sexuality & Gender and the Theology in the Raw ministry full-time.
- His podcast has grown substantially **after** leaving the institution, not during his tenure there.

**Assessment:** This is a notable **counter-example** — a faculty member with a growing public platform was unable to translate that into enrollment for a small Bible college. Contributing factors likely include EBC's ultra-low-cost model ($175/credit), geographic limitations (Boise market), and the nature of Bible college vs. seminary audiences. It suggests that **a podcast alone cannot overcome structural enrollment barriers** such as location, institutional scale, and market demand.

### NT Wright — University of St Andrews

NT Wright served as Research Professor at St Andrews (Scotland) before moving to Wycliffe Hall, Oxford in 2019. His *Ask NT Wright Anything* podcast (produced by Premier Christianity) has a significant following.

**Assessment:** No public data connects the podcast to St Andrews enrollment. Wright's role was a research professorship, not a teaching-focused position, which limits the enrollment channel. His departure to Oxford may have shifted any halo effect.

### Robert Chao Romero — UCLA

Dr. Romero is Associate Professor of Latina/o Studies at UCLA and author of *Brown Church*. He directs the Brown Church Institute and appears frequently on theology-adjacent podcasts (Fuller Studio, Yale Center for Faith & Culture, etc.).

**Assessment:** Romero does not host his own podcast. His media presence is as a guest, not a host. No evidence of enrollment impact at UCLA was found. His profile may elevate awareness of Latina/o Studies more broadly, but UCLA's enrollment dynamics are driven by factors far beyond any single professor's media presence.

## IV. KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR INSTITUTIONAL LEADERS

1. **The evidence gap is itself a finding.** Despite dozens of seminaries and Christian universities producing podcasts, and despite multiple high-profile faculty podcasters, no institution has publicly documented a causal link between podcasting and enrollment. This likely reflects a **measurement gap** rather than a lack of impact — institutions are not yet systematically tracking podcast-to-enrollment pipelines.

2. **Brand awareness is the clearest benefit.** Kate Bowler's 18 million downloads and mainstream media presence have made Duke Divinity visible to audiences who would never encounter the school otherwise. This "top of funnel" awareness is difficult to quantify in enrollment terms but is strategically valuable, particularly for schools competing in a declining market for MDiv students.

3. **Peer-reviewed research supports the mechanism.** The Cistulli & Snyder study demonstrates that podcast exposure positively influences attitudes toward universities and intentions to consider enrollment. The medium works — the question is execution and measurement.

4. **A podcast cannot overcome structural barriers.** Preston Sprinkle's experience at Eternity Bible College shows that even a growing audience cannot compensate for geographic limitations, small institutional scale, or weak market demand. Podcasting is a multiplier, not a foundation.

5. **The "celebrity professor" effect is real but unquantified.** Institutions like Western Seminary (Tim Mackie), Biola (Ed Stetzer), and Duke Divinity (Kate Bowler) clearly leverage prominent faculty in their marketing. The signal is in hiring patterns and promotional materials — schools are investing in public-platform scholars because they believe it matters, even if they haven't published the data.

6. **First-mover advantage may exist for schools that measure and publish results.** An institution that systematically tracks how prospective students discover the school — including through faculty or institutional podcasts — and publishes those findings would have a compelling case study in a field where none currently exists.

## Recommended Next Steps

- **Add "podcast / faculty media" as a tracked discovery channel** in admissions intake surveys
- **Benchmark prospective student awareness** before and after a podcast launch or faculty media push
- **Study the Northern Seminary natural experiment** — monitor whether inquiries or enrollment shift following Scot McKnight's 2024 departure
- **Review the Cistulli & Snyder study** for methodological guidance on measuring podcast impact internally
- **Engage CCCU or ATS** about collaborative research on digital content and theological education enrollment
